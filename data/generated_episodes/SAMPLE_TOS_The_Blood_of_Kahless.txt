**STAR TREK**

"The Blood of Kahless"

Written by the Writers' Room
Based on STAR TREK created by Gene Roddenberry

---

TEASER

FADE IN:

Captain's Log, Stardate 5847.3. The Enterprise is conducting a routine survey of the Verath sector when we receive a fragmented distress call from Verath IV, a Federation agricultural colony established twelve years ago. The message is unclear, but long-range sensors detect unusual energy signatures in the system—including what appears to be a Klingon vessel. Given the delicate state of Federation-Klingon relations, I've ordered the ship to yellow alert and increased speed to maximum warp. What we find there may test not only our diplomatic skills, but our understanding of the very nature of belief itself.

FADE OUT.

---

**ACT I**

FADE IN:

INT. BRIDGE - DAY

(The Bridge operates in routine efficiency. SPOCK stands at his science station, scanning data. UHURA monitors her communications board with focused attention. KIRK sits in the command chair, reviewing a data pad.)

UHURA
Captain, I'm picking up a transmission on emergency frequencies. Very weak signal, sir.

KIRK
Source?

UHURA
Verath system. Colony Verath IV.

KIRK
(setting down the pad)
Let's hear it, Lieutenant.

(UHURA works her controls. Static fills the Bridge, punctuated by broken fragments of a woman's voice.)

VOICE (V.O.)
(filtered, breaking up)
...request immediate... Federation vessel... situation has become... cannot guarantee...

(The transmission dissolves into static. UHURA adjusts frequencies.)

UHURA
That's all I can recover, sir. The signal appears to have been terminated at the source.

KIRK
Terminated? Or cut off?

UHURA
Unknown, Captain. The transmission ended abruptly.

KIRK
Mister Spock.

(SPOCK turns from his station.)

SPOCK
Verath IV. Class M planet, Federation agricultural colony established fourteen years ago. Population at last census, four hundred twelve. Primary exports: grain varieties adapted for hostile climates, botanical research specimens.

KIRK
Agricultural colony sending a distress call. What else do we have on them?

SPOCK
(consulting his scanner)
Curiously little, Captain. Starfleet records indicate the colony submitted only minimum required status reports for the past thirty-seven months. No visitors logged. No supply requests beyond essential medical provisions.

KIRK
Three years of silence from a Federation colony.

SPOCK
Not silence precisely. They have maintained technical compliance with reporting requirements. However, the reports contain no substantive information beyond confirmation of colony viability.

KIRK
That's bureaucratic silence, Mister Spock. Someone filing forms to avoid questions.

SPOCK
An accurate characterization.

UHURA
Captain, I'm detecting something else. Subspace communications traffic in the Verath sector. Encoded transmissions.

KIRK
Starfleet?

UHURA
(frowning at her board)
No, sir. The encryption pattern is Klingon.

(KIRK rises from his chair, crossing toward the viewscreen.)

KIRK
Klingon transmissions near a Federation colony.

SPOCK
The Verath system lies within Federation space, Captain. However, it is located approximately four point seven light years from the nearest disputed border region.

KIRK
Close enough for Klingon interest.

SPOCK
Evidently.

KIRK
Can you determine the number of vessels, Lieutenant?

UHURA
The traffic pattern suggests at least one ship, possibly two. They're not attempting to mask their presence, sir.

KIRK
Curious. Klingons usually prefer surprise.

SPOCK
Unless they wish their presence to be known.

KIRK
A statement rather than an infiltration.

(KIRK turns to face the viewscreen, hands clasped behind his back.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
What would the Klingons want with an agricultural colony?

SPOCK
Unknown. The planet possesses no strategic value. Mineral surveys indicate standard deposits insufficient for military interest. The colony's research has been confined to agricultural adaptation techniques.

UHURA
Captain, I'm receiving additional fragments from the same transmission. The computer has reconstructed portions from the carrier wave echoes.

KIRK
Put it through.

VOICE (V.O.)
(filtered, clearer now)
...they arrived three days ago... making demands we cannot... the artifacts belong to all of us now... please, someone must understand what we've built here...

(Static overwhelms the voice again.)

KIRK
Artifacts?

SPOCK
(raising an eyebrow)
The colony's charter makes no mention of archaeological activity. Their mandate was purely agricultural.

KIRK
Mandates change. People change.

SPOCK
Indeed. However, the failure to report archaeological discoveries of any significance would constitute a serious violation of Federation scientific protocols.

KIRK
Unless they had reason to keep their discoveries private.

SPOCK
Such secrecy would be illogical. The Federation provides substantial support for legitimate archaeological research.

KIRK
Logic doesn't always govern human choices, Spock. Fear does. Or hope. Or the need to protect something precious.

SPOCK
You are speculating, Captain.

KIRK
I'm wondering, Mister Spock. There's a difference.

(KIRK returns to his chair but does not sit.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
Lieutenant Uhura, can you raise the colony directly?

UHURA
I've been attempting contact since receiving the distress signal, sir. No response on any frequency. Their communication array appears to be intact but unmanned.

KIRK
Or they're choosing not to answer.

UHURA
Possibly, Captain.

KIRK
What about those Klingon vessels? Can you intercept their transmissions?

UHURA
I can isolate the frequency, sir, but the encryption would take considerable time to break.

SPOCK
Captain, I should note that the Klingon presence, combined with the colony's extended isolation, suggests the possibility of prior contact between the two parties.

KIRK
You think the colonists have been dealing with the Klingons?

SPOCK
I offer it as one hypothesis. The distress call mentioned demands. Demands imply negotiation, which implies a relationship of some duration.

KIRK
Three years of minimal contact with Starfleet. Klingon transmissions in the sector. A distress call mentioning artifacts.

(KIRK finally sits, his expression troubled.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
What have those colonists gotten themselves into?

UHURA
Sir, the Klingon traffic has increased. I'm reading what appears to be coordination signals between two vessels.

KIRK
Battle coordination?

UHURA
Unclear, Captain. But the pattern is consistent with ships preparing for combined operations.

KIRK
Helm, set course for Verath IV. Warp factor six.

HELMSMAN (O.S.)
Aye, sir. Course laid in.

KIRK
Engage.

(The familiar hum of the engines rises.)

SPOCK
Captain, at warp six we will arrive in approximately seven point three hours. If the Klingons are indeed preparing military action, that time frame may be insufficient.

KIRK
Then we'd better hope they're in the mood to talk before they start shooting.

SPOCK
Klingons rarely demonstrate such patience when they believe their interests are threatened.

KIRK
No. They don't.

(KIRK stares at the viewscreen, the stars now streaking past.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
But four hundred colonists don't have anyone else coming, Mister Spock. We're what they've got.

SPOCK
Understood, Captain.

UHURA
Sir, should I notify Starfleet Command of our course change?

KIRK
Compose a message, Lieutenant. Include everything we know. The distress call, the Klingon presence, the colony's isolation. Request any additional information Starfleet may have on Verath IV's recent activities.

UHURA
Aye, sir.

KIRK
And Lieutenant?

UHURA
Sir?

KIRK
Note in the message that we may be entering a diplomatically sensitive situation. I want Starfleet aware that whatever's happening out there, it wasn't started by this ship.

UHURA
Understood, Captain.

(UHURA begins composing the message. SPOCK moves from his station to stand beside the command chair.)

SPOCK
(lowering his voice)
Captain, the reference to artifacts in the transmission troubles me.

KIRK
Go on.

SPOCK
The Klingon Empire places considerable importance on objects of historical and spiritual significance. The recovery of such items from non-Klingon possession has, on several occasions, led to military action they consider justified regardless of interstellar law.

KIRK
You're suggesting these colonists may have found Klingon artifacts.

SPOCK
I am suggesting it as a possibility that would explain both the Klingon presence and the colony's secrecy.

KIRK
If Federation citizens have been hiding Klingon relics for three years, the Empire won't see it as archaeology. They'll see it as theft.

SPOCK
Precisely.

KIRK
(grimly)
And theft from Klingons is answered one way.

SPOCK
It would be logical to prepare for that eventuality.

KIRK
Logical and necessary.

(KIRK activates the intercom on his chair arm.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
Kirk to Security.

SECURITY OFFICER (V.O.)
Security here, Captain.

KIRK
Have a full landing party standing by. Twelve personnel, armed and ready to beam down on my order.

SECURITY OFFICER (V.O.)
Aye, sir. Standing by.

(KIRK closes the channel and turns back to the viewscreen.)

KIRK
Seven hours, Mister Spock.

SPOCK
Seven point three, Captain.

KIRK
Let's hope that's enough time to figure out what we're walking into.

(SPOCK returns to his station. UHURA continues monitoring frequencies. KIRK remains in his chair, watching the stars stream past, his jaw set with determination.)

FADE OUT.

INT. COLONY SETTLEMENT - MAIN SQUARE - DAY

(The transporter effect fades as KIRK, SPOCK, and MCCOY materialize in the center of a settlement that feels distinctly out of place. The architecture is standard Federation colonial prefab, but every surface has been modified—Klingon glyphs painted on walls, crossed bat'leths mounted above doorways, ceremonial banners bearing the trefoil symbol hanging from poles. A group of CHILDREN sits in a semicircle before an elder, their voices raised in rhythmic chanting.)

CHILDREN
(in unison, reciting)
"Kahless looked upon the river of blood and saw not death, but the reflection of tomorrow—"

(KIRK exchanges a glance with SPOCK. MCCOY's expression darkens immediately.)

MCCOY
Jim, am I seeing what I think I'm seeing?

KIRK
You are, Bones.

(A woman in her fifties approaches, wearing a modified Federation jumpsuit with Klingon sash across her chest. This is ADMINISTRATOR ELENA VASQUEZ, her bearing proud but welcoming.)

VASQUEZ
Captain Kirk. We received your hail. I'm Administrator Vasquez.

KIRK
Administrator. Starfleet sent us to conduct a routine welfare check. I have to say... this isn't what the reports led us to expect.

VASQUEZ
(slight smile)
The reports are twelve years old, Captain. Much has changed.

SPOCK
(scanning with tricorder)
Fascinating. These structural modifications incorporate Klingon acoustic principles. The arrangement creates resonance patterns consistent with traditional Klingon meditation chambers.

VASQUEZ
You have a discerning eye, Commander Spock. Or ear, as it were.

MCCOY
Administrator, those children are reciting Klingon poetry. War poetry, if I'm not mistaken.

VASQUEZ
The Unforgetting, Doctor. One of Kahless's meditations on memory and purpose. Hardly a war chant.

MCCOY
They're human children.

VASQUEZ
They're our children. And they're learning wisdom.

(An uncomfortable beat. KIRK steps forward, diplomatic but probing.)

KIRK
Administrator Vasquez, perhaps you could explain how this... transformation came about. Your colony was established as an agricultural settlement.

VASQUEZ
And we still grow crops, Captain. But ten years ago, a survey team found something in the northern mountains.

(She gestures for them to follow. They walk through the square, passing colonists who pause to observe—some with curiosity, others with wariness. One MAN practices combat forms with a bat'leth, his movements fluid and precise.)

VASQUEZ (CONT'D)
A Klingon vessel. Ancient. Crashed perhaps two centuries ago—long before either of our peoples knew the other existed.

SPOCK
A most improbable discovery. The Klingon Empire had not developed warp capability two hundred years prior.

VASQUEZ
Their historians might disagree with you, Commander. But the ship itself wasn't the discovery that changed us.

(They reach a modest building—clearly a shrine. VASQUEZ opens the door, revealing an interior lit by flame-pots. At the center, on a simple pedestal, rests a worn metal case.)

VASQUEZ (CONT'D)
Scrolls. Seventeen of them. Hand-copied texts from what we later learned was a monastery on Boreth.

(SPOCK approaches, tricorder humming.)

SPOCK
The metallurgical composition of this container is consistent with early Klingon space age alloys. Approximately one hundred seventy to two hundred years old. Remarkable preservation.

KIRK
What's in the scrolls?

VASQUEZ
The teachings of Kahless. His words on honor, on duty, on the warrior's path to meaning. We found them, Captain. And they found us.

MCCOY
(unable to contain himself)
You found a crashed ship and decided to remake your entire culture around the enemy's religion?

VASQUEZ
The Klingons weren't our enemies then, Doctor. We'd never even seen one. We were just... lost.

KIRK
Lost?

VASQUEZ
(quieter now)
Colony Verath IV was a failure. Crop blight the second year. Equipment failures the third. By year four, we'd lost nineteen people to accidents and illness. Starfleet promised resupply that took eighteen months to arrive. We were alone out here, Captain. Dying slowly. And then we found these words.

(She lifts the case lid, revealing aged scrolls with Klingon script.)

VASQUEZ (CONT'D)
"Death is not the enemy. A life without purpose is the enemy." Kahless wrote that. And when I read it... I understood.

KIRK
Understood what?

VASQUEZ
That we'd been surviving. Not living. These teachings gave us structure. Purpose. A reason to face each day as warriors, not victims.

SPOCK
A psychological transformation through adopted philosophical framework. The phenomenon is documented in human history—societies in crisis often seek external meaning systems.

MCCOY
It's also documented that people under stress can be susceptible to all kinds of dangerous ideas.

VASQUEZ
(sharp)
Dangerous? We haven't had a suicide in nine years, Doctor. Before we found Kahless, we had three in one month. Our children grow up with discipline, with codes of honor, with purpose. Is that dangerous?

(MCCOY has no immediate answer. KIRK studies VASQUEZ with new interest.)

KIRK
You said "warrior's path." I notice the combat training in your square. The weapons.

VASQUEZ
We settle disputes through ritual challenge, yes. No one has ever been seriously injured. The forms are... symbolic.

SPOCK
A fascinating cultural adoption. You have essentially recreated Klingon social structures without direct Klingon influence.

VASQUEZ
We had texts, Commander. We had no teachers. So yes—we interpreted. We adapted. We made it our own.

MCCOY
But it isn't your own. It belongs to the Klingons. Their history, their heroes, their... their blood rituals.

VASQUEZ
(turning to him)
Does wisdom have a race, Doctor? If a human reads Surak's teachings, do they belong less to him because he isn't Vulcan?

(SPOCK raises an eyebrow, clearly intrigued by the argument.)

SPOCK
An apt comparison. Many humans have studied Vulcan philosophy with considerable insight.

MCCOY
That's different.

SPOCK
Is it?

(Before MCCOY can respond, a YOUNG MAN rushes into the shrine—TOMAS, early twenties, wearing the same hybrid Federation-Klingon attire.)

TOMAS
Administrator! Sensors are detecting a vessel entering orbit. Configuration unknown.

(KIRK immediately taps his communicator.)

KIRK
Kirk to Enterprise.

UHURA (V.O.)
(filtered)
Enterprise here, Captain.

KIRK
Status report, Lieutenant.

UHURA (V.O.)
Sir, a vessel has just decloaked in high orbit. It's... Captain, it's a Klingon battlecruiser.

(The words hang in the air. VASQUEZ's face goes pale, then hardens with resolve.)

MCCOY
Jim...

KIRK
Raise shields. Do not—repeat, do not—power weapons unless fired upon. Kirk out.

(He turns to VASQUEZ, his voice low and urgent.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
Administrator, I need to know right now: have you had any contact with the Klingon Empire?

VASQUEZ
None. Never. We've been... we've been afraid to. We didn't know how they would react.

SPOCK
A logical concern. Klingon responses to cultural appropriation—particularly of religious significance—are historically... severe.

MCCOY
You mean they'll see it as desecration.

VASQUEZ
It isn't desecration. We honor Kahless. We live his teachings.

KIRK
That may not matter to them.

(Outside, colonists have gathered, looking skyward with a mixture of fear and something else—reverence, perhaps. The children have stopped their recitation.)

TOMAS
(to Vasquez)
What do we do?

VASQUEZ
(straightening her sash)
We do what warriors do. We face them with honor.

KIRK
Administrator, I strongly advise you let me handle first contact here.

VASQUEZ
They're coming for us, Captain. Whether you handle it or not.

(KIRK's communicator chirps.)

UHURA (V.O.)
Captain, we're receiving a transmission from the Klingon vessel. They're demanding to speak with you.

KIRK
On what frequency?

UHURA (V.O.)
All of them, sir. They're also broadcasting to the colony surface.

(A crackle of static, and then a deep, commanding voice fills the air—somehow transmitted through the colony's own speaker system.)

KOR (V.O.)
This is Commander Kor of the Imperial Klingon Defense Force. I address the thieves and defilers who have stolen the sacred blood of Kahless. You will surrender your leaders for judgment, or you will face the consequences that all desecrators deserve.

(The transmission ends. The silence that follows is deafening.)

MCCOY
(quietly)
Jim... they're calling for executions.

VASQUEZ
(voice steady, but afraid)
"Face death as a warrior faces an enemy—with respect, without fear, and never from behind."

SPOCK
Kahless?

VASQUEZ
Scroll seven. Verse fourteen.

(KIRK looks at her—this woman who quotes Klingon scripture like a believer—then up at the sky where death waits in orbit.)

KIRK
Commander Kor isn't known for his patience. We have very little time.

(He moves toward the door, then pauses, looking back at the shrine with its sacred scrolls.)

KIRK (CONT'D)
Administrator... whatever happens, I need to understand something. Do your people truly believe? Or is this just... tradition?

VASQUEZ
(meeting his eyes)
Captain Kirk, we've lived these teachings for ten years. We've raised our children in them. We've buried our dead by them. Belief isn't a question anymore. It's simply who we are.

(KIRK considers this. MCCOY looks troubled. SPOCK's expression is unreadable.)

KIRK
Then we have a problem. Because Commander Kor is about to tell you that who you are... isn't possible.

(He exits. SPOCK and MCCOY follow, leaving

END OF ACT I

**ACT II**

FADE IN:

INT. BRIDGE - DAY

    (The bridge crew operates at battle stations. Warning lights
    flash on several consoles. KIRK sits in the command chair,
    SPOCK at his science station, UHURA at communications, SCOTT
    at the engineering station.)

                         UHURA
          Captain, we're being hailed by the Klingon
          vessel. Commander Kor demands to speak with
          you directly.

                         KIRK
          On screen, Lieutenant.

    (The viewscreen flickers to life, revealing COMMANDER KOR—a
    fierce Klingon warrior with sharp features and burning eyes.
    Behind him, the dark interior of a Klingon battle cruiser.)

                         KOR
          Kirk. I should have known Starfleet would
          send you to protect their thieves.

                         KIRK
          Commander Kor. This is an unexpected
          reunion. Perhaps you'd care to explain why
          a Klingon warship has entered Federation
          space without authorization.

                         KOR
          Authorization? You dare speak to me of
          authorization while your colonists defile
          the resting place of Korath, son of Molor?

                         SPOCK
               (quietly, to Kirk)
          Captain, Korath was a legendary
          warrior-priest of the early Empire.
          Historical records suggest his vessel
          disappeared approximately three hundred
          years ago.

                         KOR
          I heard that, Vulcan. Yes. Korath's ship
          crashed on that world centuries before your
          Federation existed. His bones lie in that
          soil. His sacred teachings were meant for
          Klingon souls alone.

                         KIRK
          Commander, the colonists on Verath Four
          are Federation citizens engaged in
          legitimate settlement activities. They've
          committed no crime.

                         KOR
               (leaning forward, furious)
          No crime? They have stolen what cannot be
          stolen! They speak the words of Kahless
          with human tongues. They perform the
          rituals of our ancestors as though they
          had earned the right. They have taken
          Klingon souls and twisted them into
          something... obscene.

                         KIRK
          I've met these people, Kor. Whatever
          they've done, they've done with genuine
          reverence.

                         KOR
          Reverence? A targ may reverence the
          warrior who feeds it. That does not make
          the targ a warrior!

    (Kor stands, his hand moving to the disruptor at his hip.)

                         KOR (CONT'D)
          You will surrender the colonists to Klingon
          justice. All of them. They will stand
          before the High Council and answer for
          their desecration.

                         KIRK
          And if I refuse?

                         KOR
          Then I will take them. And your ship will
          burn in orbit as a warning to any who
          would steal from the Empire again.

    (Scott checks his instruments, his expression grim.)

                         SCOTT
               (low voice)
          Captain, I've completed my analysis of the
          Klingon vessel. She's a D-7 class, fully
          armed. If we engage, we'll give as good
          as we get, but the cost would be...

                         KIRK
          Understood, Mister Scott.
               (to Kor)
          Commander, you're asking me to hand over
          two hundred Federation citizens without
          due process. Even if I were inclined to
          do so, Starfleet Command would never
          authorize it.

                         KOR
          Then contact your Starfleet Command, Kirk.
          Tell them what is at stake. Tell them that
          the blood of Kahless himself cries out for
          justice. Tell them that if they do not
          comply, the Empire will consider this an
          act of war.

                         SPOCK
          Commander, may I point out that the
          colonists' actions, however offensive to
          Klingon sensibilities, fall within
          established precedents for cultural
          exchange. The study and adoption of
          foreign philosophical systems is not
          prohibited under any treaty between our
          governments.

                         KOR
          This is not philosophy, Vulcan! This is
          the soul of my people! Would you stand
          idle if humans dressed in Vulcan robes
          and pretended to the disciplines of Surak?

                         SPOCK
          An intriguing hypothetical. I believe I
          would find such an attempt... fascinating.

                         KOR
          You mock me.

                         SPOCK
          I assure you, Commander, mockery is not
          my intention. I merely observe that ideas,
          once expressed, cannot be unlearned.

                         KOR
               (turning back to Kirk)
          You have one of your hours, Kirk. When
          that time expires, I will have my answer.
          Surrender or battle. Those are your only
          choices.

                         KIRK
          And if I need more time to investigate
          the situation? Your accusations are
          serious, Kor. Don't I deserve the
          opportunity to determine what actually
          happened here?

    (Kor considers this, his eyes narrowing.)

                         KOR
          You would go back to the surface? Speak
          with the thieves yourself?

                         KIRK
          I would.

                         KOR
          Then go. But understand this—I know your
          tricks, Kirk. If you attempt to evacuate
          the colony, if you try to run, I will
          destroy your ship without hesitation.
          And I will hunt down every transport,
          every shuttle, every escape pod. There
          will be no survivors.

                         KIRK
          Understood.

                         KOR
          One hour. Use it wisely.

    (The screen goes dark. Kirk rises from his chair.)

                         SCOTT
          Captain, he's not bluffing. That ship
          has enough firepower to make good on
          every threat he's made.

                         KIRK
          I know, Scotty.

                         UHURA
          Sir, I've been trying to reach Starfleet
          Command, but there's heavy subspace
          interference in this sector. It may take
          time to establish a clear channel.

                         KIRK
          Keep trying, Lieutenant. Priority one.
          The Federation Council needs to know
          we're sitting on a potential flashpoint.

                         UHURA
          Aye, sir.

                         SPOCK
          Captain, may I accompany you to the
          surface? If Kor's claims regarding
          Korath are accurate, the archaeological
          evidence may prove significant.

                         KIRK
          I was counting on it, Mister Spock.
               (to Scott)
          Scotty, you have the conn. Keep shields
          up and weapons ready, but don't give
          Kor any excuse to fire first.

                         SCOTT
          Aye, Captain. And if he fires anyway?

                         KIRK
          Then defend the ship. But try to stay
          alive long enough for me to find another
          way out of this.

                         SCOTT
          I'll do my best, sir.

    (Kirk moves toward the turbolift. Spock joins him.)

                         KIRK
          Spock, what do you know about this
          Korath?

                         SPOCK
          Very little, Captain. He is considered
          a figure of some religious significance,
          though the details of his teachings have
          never been shared with outsiders. If the
          colonists have somehow gained access to
          those teachings...

                         KIRK
          Then we may have stepped into something
          bigger than a territorial dispute.

                         SPOCK
          Considerably bigger, I would estimate.

    (They enter the turbolift.)

                         KIRK
          Transporter room.

    (The doors close.)

                                                      FADE OUT.

INT. KLINGON SHRINE - VERATH IV - NIGHT

    (A stone structure built into a hillside, lit by 
    flickering torches. Klingon glyphs are carved into 
    the walls with evident care. At the center stands a 
    crude but dignified altar bearing a bat'leth replica. 
    ELENA VASQUEZ kneels before it in meditation. KIRK, 
    SPOCK, and MCCOY enter. Vasquez rises, facing them 
    without apology.)

                    KIRK
        Administrator Vasquez. We need to talk.

                    VASQUEZ
        I wondered when you'd find your way 
        here, Captain.

                    KIRK
        Your people built this?

                    VASQUEZ
        With our own hands. Every stone 
        carried from the quarry. Every glyph 
        learned and carved correctly.

                    MCCOY
        It's... impressive work.

                    VASQUEZ
        You sound surprised, Doctor.

                    MCCOY
              (uncomfortable)
        I suppose I am.

    (Kirk moves along the wall, studying the inscriptions. 
    His fingers trace one of the carvings.)

                    KIRK
        Administrator, Commander Kor has 
        a battlecruiser in orbit. He's 
        speaking of desecration, of war. 
        I need to understand what happened 
        here. All of it.

                    VASQUEZ
        You want to know why we risked 
        everything?

                    KIRK
        Yes.

    (Vasquez moves to the altar, her hand resting on the 
    bat'leth replica.)

                    VASQUEZ
        When we arrived here twelve years 
        ago, Verath IV was supposed to be 
        paradise. Rich soil, temperate 
        climate, everything the surveys 
        promised.

                    SPOCK
        The colonization reports were 
        favorable.

                    VASQUEZ
        Reports don't tell you about the 
        blight that kills your crops three 
        seasons running. They don't mention 
        the geological instability that 
        destroys your water reclamation 
        systems. They don't prepare you 
        for watching children go hungry.

                    MCCOY
        You were struggling.

                    VASQUEZ
        We were dying, Doctor. Slowly. 
        Not from any single catastrophe, 
        but from a thousand small failures. 
        People started talking about 
        abandoning the colony. Requesting 
        evacuation.

                    KIRK
        But you didn't.

                    VASQUEZ
        No. Because Marcus Chen found the 
        caves.

    (She gestures toward the shrine's deeper chamber.)

                    VASQUEZ (CONT'D)
        Ancient artifacts. Weapons, 
        ceremonial objects, and scrolls. 
        Klingon, but old. Very old. From 
        before their empire spread this 
        far.

                    SPOCK
        Fascinating. Pre-imperial Klingon 
        artifacts would be of considerable 
        archaeological significance.

                    VASQUEZ
        They were more than artifacts, 
        Mister Spock. The scrolls contained 
        teachings. The words of Kahless 
        himself, or close enough. About 
        honor. Struggle. The warrior's 
        duty to face death rather than 
        accept defeat.

                    KIRK
        And you read them.

                    VASQUEZ
        I translated them. Night after 
        night, while the colony fell apart 
        around us. And something changed 
        in me.

    (She turns to face Kirk directly, her eyes bright 
    with conviction.)

                    VASQUEZ (CONT'D)
        Kahless teaches that a warrior 
        finds purpose not in victory, but 
        in the struggle itself. That there 
        is no honor in surrender. That 
        you face your enemy—whether it's 
        an army or a failed harvest—and 
        you fight until you win or die.

                    MCCOY
        That's... not what I expected from 
        Klingon philosophy.

                    VASQUEZ
        What did you expect, Doctor? 
        Bloodlust? Savagery?

                    MCCOY
              (quietly)
        I suppose I did.

                    VASQUEZ
        Kahless was a teacher. A unifier. 
        Yes, he was a warrior—but his 
        teachings speak of honor, loyalty, 
        duty to one's people. We were 
        colonists with no direction, no 
        purpose beyond survival. He gave 
        us something to believe in.

                    KIRK
        And the colony recovered.

                    VASQUEZ
        We rebuilt. We stopped waiting for 
        rescue and started acting like 
        warriors. When the equipment failed, 
        we learned to forge our own tools. 
        When the crops died, we found new 
        ways to grow food. We stopped being 
        victims.

                    SPOCK
        A logical response to adversity, 
        regardless of its philosophical 
        framework.

                    VASQUEZ
        Perhaps. But the framework matters, 
        Mister Spock. It gave us identity. 
        Community. Something larger than 
        ourselves to serve.

    (Kirk walks to the altar, looking at the bat'leth.)

                    KIRK
        You knew the Klingons would find 
        out eventually.

                    VASQUEZ
        We knew it was possible.

                    KIRK
        And you did it anyway.

                    VASQUEZ
        Captain, when you find something 
        true—something that transforms your 
        people from desperate refugees into 
        a community with purpose and pride—
        do you abandon it because it might 
        be dangerous?

                    KIRK
        Most people would.

                    VASQUEZ
        Kahless teaches that fear is the 
        enemy of honor. We chose not to 
        be afraid.

    (A long moment of silence. McCoy moves closer to 
    the altar, studying the craftsmanship.)

                    MCCOY
        Administrator, when I first heard 
        about this... I thought you were 
        fools. Playing at something you 
        didn't understand.

                    VASQUEZ
        Many would agree with you.

                    MCCOY
        I was wrong.

    (Vasquez looks at him with surprise.)

                    MCCOY (CONT'D)
        I've spent my career believing that 
        compassion, gentleness—these are 
        the highest virtues. And they are. 
        But there's more than one kind of 
        strength. What you've built here... 
        it's real.

                    VASQUEZ
        Thank you, Doctor.

                    SPOCK
        Doctor McCoy's observation is 
        accurate. The colonists' dedication 
        to Klingon principles demonstrates 
        disciplined commitment comparable 
        to practitioners of any established 
        faith tradition.

                    KIRK
        Spock?

                    SPOCK
        Captain, I have observed the 
        colonists' adherence to these 
        teachings. Their practices are 
        not imitation or affectation. 
        They have genuinely internalized 
        the philosophical framework and 
        live according to its precepts. 
        By any measurable standard, their 
        devotion is authentic.

                    VASQUEZ
        Will that matter to Commander Kor?

                    KIRK
        I don't know.

    (Kirk turns away from the altar, pacing. His mind 
    is working.)

                    KIRK (CONT'D)
        Administrator, tell me about 
        Klingon law. Specifically regarding 
        matters of honor.

                    VASQUEZ
        That's... a broad subject, Captain.

                    KIRK
        The teachings of Kahless. Do they 
        address who may follow them?

                    VASQUEZ
        I don't understand.

                    KIRK
        Is there anything in the scrolls, 
        the ceremonies, the traditions—
        anything that explicitly limits 
        the way of Kahless to those of 
        Klingon blood?

    (Vasquez considers this carefully.)

                    VASQUEZ
        Kahless spoke of warriors. Of 
        those with honor. He never... no. 
        No, he never specifically addressed 
        the question.

                    SPOCK
        An interesting omission.

                    KIRK
        More than interesting, Spock. 
        If Klingon spiritual tradition 
        doesn't explicitly exclude 
        non-Klingons...

                    MCCOY
        Then Kor can't claim they've 
        violated it.

                    KIRK
        It's more than that, Bones.

    (Kirk turns back to Vasquez, energy in his voice 
    now.)

                    KIRK (CONT'D)
        Administrator, Klingons respect 
        strength. Honor. Courage in the 
        face of death.

                    VASQUEZ
        Above all things.

                    KIRK
        Then we don't defend your colony 
        by citing Federation law. We don't 
        make this about territory or 
        treaties.

                    SPOCK
        Captain, Starfleet regulations 
        clearly establish—

                    KIRK
        Regulations won't stop Kor from 
        opening fire, Spock. But his own 
        principles might.

                    MCCOY
        What are you thinking, Jim?

                    KIRK
        I'm thinking that Commander Kor 
        considers himself a man of honor. 
        He follows the teachings of Kahless 
        as surely as these colonists do. 
        If we can demonstrate that your 
        people are genuine followers—not 
        thieves, not pretenders—

                    VASQUEZ
        He would have to acknowledge us.

                    KIRK
        He would have to judge you by 
        Klingon standards. By the standards 
        of honor he claims to serve.

                    SPOCK
        An intriguing strategy, Captain. 
        However, the application of Klingon 
        spiritual law to non-Klingons has 
        no precedent.

END OF ACT II

**ACT III**

FADE IN:

INT. COLONY CENTRAL SQUARE - DAY

    (The dusty square is ringed by simple stone buildings bearing
    Klingon glyphs alongside Federation colonial markers. COLONISTS
    stand in ranks—men, women, children—facing the center with the
    rigid discipline of warriors awaiting battle. Some wear crude
    armor fashioned from local materials. Others grip farming
    implements like weapons.)

    (KIRK materializes with SPOCK, MCCOY, and SCOTT. Beside them,
    Commander KOR shimmers into existence, flanked by two KLINGON
    WARRIORS. Kor's hand rests on his disruptor as he surveys the
    assembled humans with barely concealed contempt.)

                         KOR
    So. You bring me to witness your thieves
    in their stolen garments.

                         KIRK
    I bring you to meet people who may
    surprise you, Commander.

    (GOVERNOR THAL steps forward from the colonists' ranks. She is
    weathered, proud, bearing a bat'leth across her back.)

                         THAL
    Commander Kor. I am Thal, daughter of
    Marcus. I speak for Verath IV.

                         KOR
              (sneering)
    You speak Klingon words with a human
    tongue. It means nothing.

                         THAL
    Words are wind. Judge us by our deeds.

    (Kor circles her slowly, predatory. The Klingon warriors fan
    out, hands near weapons.)

                         KOR
    Your deeds? You have taken what does not
    belong to you. The sacred texts. The
    rituals of Kahless. These are the
    inheritance of the Klingon people.

                         MCCOY
              (quietly, to Kirk)
    Jim, this isn't going to work. He's not
    listening.

                         KIRK
    Give it time, Bones.

                         SPOCK
    The Commander's posture suggests genuine
    religious conviction rather than mere
    political posturing. Fascinating.

                         KOR
              (to Thal)
    Tell me, human—what do you know of
    Sto-vo-kor? Of the Black Fleet? Of the
    honor that awaits those who die in glory?

                         THAL
    I know that Kahless said the gates of
    Sto-vo-kor are open to all who live with
    honor and die with courage. He did not
    say "all Klingons." He said all.

    (Kor's eyes narrow. One of his warriors growls.)

                         KOR
    You twist the words of our greatest
    warrior to serve your theft.

                         KIRK
    Commander, I'd like to ask you something.

    (Kirk steps between Kor and Thal, drawing the Klingon's
    attention.)

                         KOR
    You test my patience, Kirk.

                         KIRK
    Indulge me. One question. What makes a
    warrior?

                         KOR
    Blood. Heritage. The fire that burns in
    Klingon hearts.

                         KIRK
    Is that what Kahless taught? That
    warrior spirit is an accident of birth?

    (Kor's jaw tightens.)

                         KIRK (CONT'D)
    I've read the texts too, Commander.
    Kahless forged the first bat'leth from
    his own hair and the fires of the
    Kri'stak volcano. He wasn't born a
    legend. He made himself one. Through
    choice. Through discipline. Through
    devotion.

                         SPOCK
    The Captain's interpretation aligns with
    the earliest known Klingon religious
    manuscripts. Kahless himself was said to
    have welcomed warriors of conquered
    peoples into his personal guard—provided
    they embraced the path of honor.

                         KOR
              (to Spock)
    You presume to lecture me on my own
    history, Vulcan?

                         SPOCK
    I merely state documented fact,
    Commander. The logical conclusion is
    self-evident.

                         MCCOY
    What Spock's trying to say—in his
    roundabout way—is that your own
    traditions support what these people
    have done.

                         KOR
    My traditions? What would a human doctor
    know of Klingon traditions?

                         MCCOY
    I know that a man's faith doesn't live
    in his bloodstream. It lives in his
    heart. In his choices. And these people
    have chosen.

    (Kor turns back to the colonists. He walks among them slowly,
    examining faces. He stops before a YOUNG BOY, perhaps ten,
    standing rigid with a small blade at his belt.)

                         KOR
    You. Child. Are you prepared to die
    today?

    (The boy's mother tenses but does not move.)

                         YOUNG BOY
    If I must die, I will die standing.
    Kahless teaches that fear is the mind-
    killer. I am not afraid.

                         KOR
    And if I ordered my warriors to fire on
    your colony? To burn everything you have
    built?

                         YOUNG BOY
    Then you would prove yourself without
    honor. And we would go to Sto-vo-kor
    knowing we kept faith.

    (Kor stares at the boy for a long moment. Something shifts in
    his expression—the contempt giving way to something more
    complex.)

                         SCOTT
              (low, to Kirk)
    Captain, I'm reading power fluctuations
    from the Klingon ship. They may be
    charging weapons.

                         KIRK
              (equally low)
    Hold position, Scotty. Trust me.

    (Kirk steps forward, addressing both Kor and the colonists.)

                         KIRK (CONT'D)
    Commander Kor, you came here to punish
    thieves. But look at these people.
    Really look at them.

    (He gestures to the assembled colonists.)

                         KIRK (CONT'D)
    They didn't take your beliefs to mock
    them. They didn't study your sacred
    texts to gain advantage. They found
    something in the words of Kahless that
    spoke to their souls. Something true.
    Something worth living for. Worth dying
    for.

                         KOR
    They are not Klingon.

                         KIRK
    No. They're human. And they've chosen to
    walk the path of the warrior anyway.
    Isn't that the harder road? Isn't that
    more honorable—to embrace a difficult
    truth when the easy path of your own
    culture lies open?

    (Kor is silent. His warriors exchange uncertain glances.)

                         THAL
    Commander. My people have tended this
    ground for ten years. We have built our
    shrine with our own hands. We have
    raised our children in the ways of
    honor. Every stone here is laid in
    devotion. Every field is planted in
    service to something greater than
    ourselves.

                         KOR
    And what do you ask of me?

                         THAL
    Nothing. We make no demands. If you
    judge us unworthy, we accept our fate.
    But we will not renounce our faith. We
    would rather die as warriors than live
    as cowards.

    (A long, tense silence. Kor looks from Thal to Kirk to the
    colonists. Finally, he laughs—a short, sharp bark.)

                         KOR
    You are either the most foolish humans
    I have ever encountered... or the most
    remarkable.

                         KIRK
    I've been called both, Commander.
    Sometimes in the same sentence.

                         KOR
              (to Thal)
    I cannot call you Klingon. That is not
    within my power to grant. But I will say
    this—

    (He draws his d'k tahg. The colonists tense. Kirk's hand moves
    toward his phaser—but Kor reverses the blade, holding it in
    salute.)

                         KOR (CONT'D)
    You are honorable enemies. Worthy of
    respect. Today, there will be no blood
    on this ground.

    (Thal bows her head in acknowledgment. Kor sheathes his blade
    and turns to Kirk.)

                         KOR (CONT'D)
    Do not mistake this for peace, Kirk.
    Between our peoples, nothing has
    changed. The Empire will remember this
    colony—and we will watch.

                         KIRK
    I wouldn't expect anything less.

                         KOR
    But you have shown me something today.
    Something I did not expect from humans.
              (beat)
    Perhaps your species is not entirely
    without merit.

                         MCCOY
    High praise from a Klingon.

                         KOR
              (almost smiling)
    Do not let it go to your head, Doctor.

    (Kor signals his warriors. They move to join him.)

                         KOR (CONT'D)
    Kirk. Until we meet again.

                         KIRK
    I look forward to it, Commander.

    (Kor and his warriors shimmer out of existence. The colonists
    release a collective breath. Several embrace. The young boy's
    mother pulls him close.)

                         THAL
    Captain Kirk... I don't know how to
    thank you.

                         KIRK
    Keep doing what you're doing, Governor.
    Live with honor. That's thanks enough.

                         SCOTT
    Captain, the Klingon vessel is breaking
    orbit. They're setting course for the
    Neutral Zone.

                         KIRK
    Acknowledged, Scotty.
              (to Thal)
    The Enterprise will remain in orbit
    until we're certain they've departed.
    After that... you're on your own.

                         THAL
    We always were, Captain. That's what
    made us strong.

    (Kirk nods, a hint of admiration in his expression. He flips
    open his communicator.)

                         KIRK
    Kirk to Enterprise. Four to beam up.

    (As the transporter effect begins to shimmer around them—)

                                                    CUT TO:

END OF ACT III
