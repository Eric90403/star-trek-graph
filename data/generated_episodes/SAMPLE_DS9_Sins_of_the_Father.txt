**STAR TREK: DEEP SPACE NINE**

"Sins of the Father"

Written by the Writers' Room
Based on STAR TREK created by Gene Roddenberry

---

TEASER

FADE IN:

Captain's log, stardate 51734.2. Deep Space Nine continues to serve as a critical staging point for Alpha Quadrant forces in the Dominion War. Supply convoys arrive daily, bringing fresh troops and materiel—along with the inevitable flow of refugees, smugglers, and those seeking sanctuary from the conflict. In war, desperation makes strange bedfellows, and today's distress call may test that principle more than most. A damaged Cardassian shuttle has just been detected on long-range sensors, transmitting a priority asylum request. In these times, trust is a luxury we can rarely afford—but neither can we afford to ignore any potential advantage.

FADE OUT.

**ACT I**

FADE IN:

INT. OPERATIONS CENTER - NIGHT

(The night shift crew maintains their stations, the usual quiet hum of the station's systems providing a steady backdrop. DAX sits at the science console, running routine sensor sweeps. SISKO emerges from his office, a PADD in hand, clearly working late.)

DAX
Benjamin, you should be sleeping.

SISKO
I could say the same for you, old man.

DAX
I'm running calibrations on the long-range sensors. What's your excuse?

SISKO
(holds up the PADD)
Casualty reports from the Seventh Fleet. Admiral Ross wants my assessment by oh-six-hundred.

(DAX's console suddenly CHIRPS with an alert. She leans forward, her expression shifting to focused concern.)

DAX
I'm picking up a vessel dropping out of warp... bearing one-seven-mark-four. It's small. Shuttle-sized.

SISKO
(moving to her station)
Identification?

DAX
Cardassian. Hideki-class shuttle. She's badly damaged, Benjamin. I'm reading multiple hull breaches, failing life support...
(beat)
One life sign. Fading.

SISKO
Tractor beam. Get them into docking bay four.

DAX
(working the console)
Tractor beam engaged.
(then, with concern)
The pilot's vital signs are deteriorating. I don't think they have long.

SISKO
(tapping his combadge)
Sisko to Doctor Bashir.

BASHIR (V.O.)
(groggy)
Bashir here.

SISKO
Medical emergency, docking bay four. We're bringing in a damaged Cardassian shuttle with one survivor.

BASHIR (V.O.)
On my way.

SISKO
(to Dax)
Contact Odo. Have him meet us there.

(SISKO moves toward the turbolift. DAX follows, leaving the night shift to manage Operations.)

INT. DOCKING BAY FOUR - CONTINUOUS

(The Cardassian shuttle rests in the bay, smoke venting from multiple ruptures in its hull. Scorch marks pattern the exterior in an unmistakable weapons signature. SISKO and DAX arrive just as BASHIR rushes in with a medical kit and an anti-grav stretcher. ODO enters from another entrance, his expression immediately assessing.)

BASHIR
(scanning with tricorder)
The hatch is sealed from inside. Can we cut through?

DAX
(working a wall panel)
I can bypass the external lock... there.

(The shuttle hatch hisses open with a groan of stressed metal. Smoke billows out. BASHIR doesn't hesitate—he climbs inside.)

ODO
(examining the hull)
Commander. These scoring patterns.

SISKO
You recognize them?

ODO
Polaron-based weapons fire. Jem'Hadar.

(SISKO's jaw tightens. A moment later, BASHIR emerges, helping guide the stretcher. On it lies MIRA DUKAT, a young Cardassian woman in her early twenties. Her face is bruised, her clothing torn and bloodied. She's unconscious, breathing shallowly.)

BASHIR
Female, approximately twenty-two years old. Severe plasma burns, internal bleeding, possible cranial trauma.
(to Sisko)
I need to get her to the infirmary immediately.

SISKO
Go.

(BASHIR hurries out with the stretcher. SISKO watches him go, then turns back to the shuttle.)

SISKO (CONT'D)
Dax, I want a full analysis of that shuttle. Flight recorder, navigational data, everything.

DAX
I'll have a preliminary report within the hour.

(ODO has already moved inside the shuttle, examining the interior with methodical precision.)

ODO
Commander. You should see this.

(SISKO steps into the cramped, damaged interior. ODO gestures to a small case wedged beneath the pilot's seat, partially hidden. It's been secured with multiple locks.)

SISKO
What is it?

ODO
I don't know. But she went to considerable effort to protect it.

SISKO
(beat)
Bring it to the security office. Don't open it until I'm there.

ODO
Understood.

(SISKO takes one last look around the shuttle's interior—the desperation evident in every hastily bypassed system, every jury-rigged repair.)

SISKO
Someone wanted her dead very badly.

ODO
The Jem'Hadar don't pursue damaged shuttles unless ordered to. Whoever this woman is, the Dominion considers her worth killing.

SISKO
(grim)
Then we'd better find out why.

INT. INFIRMARY - LATER

(MIRA lies on a biobed, still unconscious. Medical monitors display her vitals—stabilizing but still critical. BASHIR works with focused intensity, a hypospray in one hand, the other adjusting settings on the biobed's neural stimulator. SISKO enters quietly, standing back to let the doctor work.)

BASHIR
(without looking up)
I've stopped the internal bleeding and treated the worst of the plasma burns. The cranial trauma was less severe than I initially thought.

SISKO
Will she survive?

BASHIR
(finally looking up)
She should make a full recovery. Though I'd say she came within ten minutes of death. If we hadn't tractored that shuttle in when we did...

(MIRA suddenly stirs, her eyes fluttering open. She tries to sit up, panicked, and BASHIR gently restrains her.)

BASHIR (CONT'D)
Easy. Easy. You're safe. You're on a Federation space station.

MIRA
(hoarse, frightened)
The Jem'Hadar—

BASHIR
They're gone. You made it.

(MIRA's eyes dart around the infirmary, taking in her surroundings. She sees SISKO, and something in her expression shifts—fear mixing with desperate hope.)

MIRA
You're... you're Commander Sisko.

SISKO
(stepping closer)
That's right. And you are?

(MIRA hesitates. A long beat. Then, with visible effort, she steels herself.)

MIRA
My name is Mira. Mira Dukat.

(The name lands like a physical blow. SISKO's expression hardens almost imperceptibly. BASHIR glances between them, recognizing the significance.)

SISKO
Dukat.

MIRA
(quickly)
I know what you're thinking. I know what he's done. What he continues to do.
(struggling to sit up)
But I'm not him. I've never been him. And right now, I need your help.

SISKO
(measured)
You'll understand if I'm skeptical.

MIRA
The Dominion is hunting me, Commander. Not because of who my father is—because of what I took from him.

BASHIR
She needs rest, Captain. Whatever this is—

MIRA
(interrupting, urgent)
I have intelligence. Dominion fleet movements. Deployment schedules. Things my father thinks are safely locked in his compound on Cardassia.
(beat)
I copied everything. And I ran.

(SISKO studies her face—looking for deception, for manipulation. What he sees is exhaustion and fear and something that might be genuine desperation.)

SISKO
Why?

MIRA
Because I've seen what they're planning. And because...
(her voice catches)
Because I couldn't be his daughter anymore. Not after everything I learned.

(ODO enters, pausing at the threshold as he takes in the scene.)

ODO
Commander. I've completed a preliminary examination of the shuttle.

SISKO
(not taking his eyes off Mira)
And?

ODO
The damage is consistent with Jem'Hadar weapons fire. Multiple attack runs over an extended pursuit—I'd estimate several hours. Whoever attacked her was persistent.
(beat)
I also found evidence of at least three separate attempts to disable her warp drive. They wanted to capture her, not destroy her.

MIRA
(to Sisko)
You see? If I were a trap, they wouldn't have tried so hard to stop me.

SISKO
Or they wanted it to look that way.

MIRA
(frustrated)
I'm risking my life coming here. I've betrayed everything I was raised to believe. Do you think I don't know what will happen if my father finds me?

ODO
(interjecting)
Mira Dukat. I've been searching our records. There's no mention of you in any Cardassian database we have access to.

MIRA
(bitter)
Of course there isn't. I'm illegitimate. A half-Cardassian embarrassment he kept hidden away. My mother was Bajoran.

(The revelation hangs in the air. BASHIR's expression shifts—professional distance giving way to something more complicated.)

SISKO
Bajoran.

MIRA
A comfort woman during the Occupation. She died when I was seven. My father... acknowledged me. In his way.
(her voice hardening)
He made sure I was educated. Fed. Housed. As long as I stayed invisible.

ODO
That would explain the lack of official records.

SISKO
(to Odo)
What about the case you found on the shuttle?

ODO
Still sealed. I was waiting for your authorization to open it.

MIRA
(alarmed)
Be careful with it. The data isolinears inside—if they're damaged, everything I went through means nothing.

SISKO
(long beat, considering)
Doctor, how long until she's fit for a full debriefing?

BASHIR
I'd recommend at least twelve hours of rest. Her body has been through significant trauma.

MIRA
(protesting)
I can talk now. I need to tell you—

BASHIR
(firmly)
What you need is to let your body heal. The intelligence will still be there in twelve hours.

(SISKO moves closer to the biobed, looking down at MIRA with an expression that's impossible to read.)

SISKO
If what you're telling me is true, you may have just handed us a significant advantage in this war.
(beat)
But I've dealt with Cardassians before. I've dealt with your father before. So you'll understand that trust is something you'll have to earn.

MIRA
(meeting his gaze)
I'm not asking you to trust me

INT. KIRA'S OFFICE - DAY

(KIRA stands behind her desk, arms crossed, facing SISKO. Her posture is rigid, defensive. SISKO remains calm but firm.)

KIRA
With all due respect, Commander, you can't be serious.

SISKO
I'm completely serious, Major.

KIRA
She's a Cardassian. Dukat's daughter. And you want me to conduct the debrief?

SISKO
I want you to conduct the debrief because you know the Occupation better than anyone on this station. You know Cardassian tactics. You know when they're lying.

KIRA
I know when they're lying because they're always lying.

SISKO
Then this should be straightforward.

(KIRA moves around her desk, agitated.)

KIRA
Commander... I spent ten years fighting Cardassians. I've seen what they do to Bajoran villages. What they do to children. And now you want me to sit across from Dukat's offspring and have a conversation?

SISKO
I want you to extract intelligence that could save lives.

KIRA
If there even is any intelligence. This could be a Dominion operation. A plant.

SISKO
It could be. That's why I need someone who won't be fooled.

(Beat. KIRA looks away, jaw tight.)

KIRA
You're asking me to treat her like a person.

SISKO
I'm asking you to do your job, Major.

(KIRA turns back to him, eyes hard.)

KIRA
And if I can't separate what she is from who her father is?

SISKO
(evenly)
Then you tell me, and I'll find someone else. But I don't think that's going to happen.

KIRA
... Why not?

SISKO
Because you've never let your feelings compromise the mission. Not once.

(The compliment lands awkwardly. KIRA doesn't know how to respond.)

KIRA
Fine. I'll do the debrief. But I'm not going to pretend to be sympathetic.

SISKO
I'm not asking you to pretend anything.

(He moves toward the door, then pauses.)

SISKO (CONT'D)
She's in the security holding area. Odo's completing his preliminary investigation now.

(KIRA nods curtly. SISKO exits.)

CUT TO:

INT. SECURITY OFFICE - CONTINUOUS

(ODO stands at his console, studying a PADD. SISKO enters. ODO looks up, his expression unreadable as always.)

ODO
Commander. I was about to contact you.

SISKO
What have you found?

ODO
(handing him the PADD)
Doctor Bashir completed his genetic analysis an hour ago. I've been... reviewing the results.

(SISKO takes the PADD, reads. His brow furrows.)

SISKO
This can't be right.

ODO
I had the same reaction. I asked the Doctor to run the analysis twice more.

SISKO
Half-Bajoran?

ODO
Her maternal DNA is unquestionably Bajoran. The markers are consistent with the Dahkur province.

(Beat.)

SISKO
How is that possible?

ODO
(dryly)
I suspect the biological mechanics are straightforward enough. The circumstances, however...

SISKO
A Bajoran woman and Gul Dukat.

ODO
During the Occupation, such... arrangements... were not unheard of.

(The weight of that statement hangs in the air.)

SISKO
Does Kira know?

ODO
Not yet.

SISKO
Don't tell her. Not until after the debrief. I want her initial assessment unclouded.

ODO
With respect, Commander, Major Kira is unlikely to find her assessment "unclouded" by any definition.

SISKO
Understood. But this information changes the equation. I need to know if Mira Dukat's story holds up before we factor in... complications.

(ODO inclines his head slightly.)

ODO
There's something else you should see.

(He taps his console. A viewscreen activates, showing security footage of MIRA DUKAT in the holding area. She sits on the bench, hunched forward, staring at nothing. Her damaged arm is now properly treated but still bandaged.)

ODO (CONT'D)
She's been like this for two hours. Hasn't eaten. Hasn't slept. She asked once about the status of her shuttle's data core, and when I told her we were still attempting to recover it, she simply... stopped speaking.

SISKO
(watching)
That's not the behavior of a Dominion operative.

ODO
No. It's the behavior of someone who's given up.

(Beat.)

SISKO
Keep watching her.

ODO
I always do.

(KIRA enters. MIRA looks up slowly, her expression wary. There's a force field between them. KIRA makes no move to lower it.)

KIRA
I'm Major Kira Nerys. I'll be conducting your debrief.

MIRA
(flatly)
The Bajoran first officer. My father mentioned you.

KIRA
(sharp)
Did he.

MIRA
He said you were dangerous.

KIRA
Your father was right about something for once.

(MIRA almost smiles. It's a bitter, exhausted expression.)

MIRA
Are you here to interrogate me or execute me? Either way, I'd appreciate knowing in advance.

KIRA
That depends on what you tell me.

(KIRA moves closer to the force field, studying MIRA like a predator assessing prey.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
Let's start with the obvious question. Why should I believe anything that comes out of your mouth?

MIRA
You shouldn't.

(Beat. KIRA wasn't expecting that.)

KIRA
Excuse me?

MIRA
I'm a Cardassian. Dukat's daughter. I was raised in privilege while your people starved. Why would you believe me?

KIRA
... I'm asking the questions.

MIRA
Then ask better ones.

(KIRA's eyes narrow.)

KIRA
Fine. The intelligence you claim to have. Where did you get it?

MIRA
My father's compound on Cardassia Prime. His personal database.

KIRA
And he just... left classified Dominion tactical data lying around?

MIRA
He trusted me.

(Something flickers across MIRA's face. Pain. Shame.)

MIRA (CONT'D)
I was his favorite. His... project.

KIRA
What does that mean?

MIRA
(looking away)
It means he told me things. Showed me things. Brought me into his confidence because he thought... he thought I was like him.

(Beat.)

KIRA
And are you?

MIRA
(meeting her eyes)
I killed two Jem'Hadar soldiers getting off Cardassia. I crippled a patrol ship in the Badlands. I flew through an asteroid field with a damaged warp core rather than let them capture me.

(Pause.)

MIRA (CONT'D)
So yes, Major. I suppose in some ways I am exactly like him.

(KIRA is unsettled by the self-awareness in MIRA's voice.)

KIRA
Why did you run? You had everything. Position, protection...

MIRA
(cutting her off)
I had a gilded cage and a monster's name.

(She stands, moving to the edge of the holding cell.)

MIRA (CONT'D)
Do you know what it's like to grow up as Dukat's daughter? To be paraded around as proof of his... enlightenment? "Look at my half-breed child," he'd say. "See how I care for the lesser species."

(KIRA stiffens.)

KIRA
Half-breed?

(MIRA realizes she's said too much. She turns away.)

MIRA
Forget it.

KIRA
No. What did you just say?

MIRA
I said forget it.

KIRA
(stepping forward)
You said half-breed. What did you mean by that?

(Long pause. MIRA doesn't turn around.)

MIRA
My mother was Bajoran.

(The words hang in the air. KIRA looks like she's been struck.)

KIRA
... That's not possible.

MIRA
(bitterly)
Isn't it? You know what the Occupation was. You know what men like my father did.

(She finally turns to face KIRA.)

MIRA (CONT'D)
My mother was a comfort woman. She died giving birth to me. My father... kept me. Raised me as a Cardassian. Hid what I was from everyone except himself.

(Beat.)

MIRA (CONT'D)
I didn't learn the truth until three years ago. And I've been living with it every day since.

(KIRA is struggling to process this. Her hatred is fighting against something else—recognition, perhaps. Horror.)

KIRA
Why should I believe any of this?

MIRA
You have a doctor. Run the scan yourself.

(Pause.)

KIRA
We already did.

(MIRA nods slowly, unsurprised.)

MIRA
Then you know I'm not lying. About that, at least.

(KIRA turns away, pacing. When she speaks again, her voice is strained.)

KIRA
The Prophets know I've seen... I've seen Cardassians do terrible things. But this...

MIRA
(quietly)
I'm not asking for your sympathy, Major. I'm asking for asylum. There's a difference.

KIRA
Is there?

(She turns back to face MIRA.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
Because from where I'm standing, you're still Dukat's daughter. You still grew up in his house, ate his food, wore his name. Half-Bajoran or not.

MIRA
I know what I am.

KIRA
Do you? Because I'm not sure I do anymore.

**ACT II**

FADE IN:

INT. QUARK'S BAR - EVENING

(The bar is quieter than usual, the dinner rush having thinned to scattered clusters of Starfleet personnel and civilians. KIRA sits alone at the far end of the bar, a half-empty glass of springwine in front of her. She's not drinking—just staring at it. DAX enters, spots Kira, and approaches with easy familiarity.)

DAX
I've been looking for you. Odo said you left Ops early.

KIRA
I'm off duty.

DAX
(sitting beside her)
I can see that.

(Kira doesn't respond. Dax signals to QUARK, who's wiping down glasses behind the bar.)

DAX (CONT'D)
Raktajino, please.

QUARK
At this hour? You'll be up all night.

DAX
That's the idea.

(Quark shrugs and moves to prepare the drink. Dax turns back to Kira, studying her profile.)

DAX (CONT'D)
You want to talk about it?

KIRA
Not particularly.

DAX
All right.

(She waits. A long beat. Kira finally exhales, shoulders dropping.)

KIRA
I went to see her. In the infirmary.

DAX
Mira Dukat.

KIRA
(bitter)
Don't call her that.

DAX
What should I call her?

KIRA
I don't know. That's the problem.

(Quark delivers Dax's raktajino, lingering a moment longer than necessary before moving away—but not far.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
I walked in there ready to... I don't know what I was ready to do. Interrogate her. Catch her in a lie. Something.

DAX
And?

KIRA
(quietly)
She flinched. When I came through the door. She flinched like I was going to hit her.

(Kira picks up her glass, sets it down again without drinking.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
Do you know what Bashir told me? Her medical records. The healed fractures, the scar tissue. It goes back years. Decades.

DAX
I saw the preliminary report.

KIRA
Then you know. You know what he did to her.

DAX
Her father.

KIRA
(sharp)
Dukat. Say his name. Dukat.

(Dax nods, accepting the correction.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
The man who ran the Occupation. The man responsible for... for everything. He had a Bajoran comfort woman—I knew that. Everyone knew. And he had a daughter with her, and he hid that daughter away like garbage.

DAX
Because acknowledging her would have ended his career.

KIRA
Because she was half-Bajoran. Because she was evidence of his... his...

(She can't find the word. Her hand tightens around the glass.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
I should hate her. I want to hate her. She's his blood. She carries his name.

DAX
But?

KIRA
(breaking)
But she grew up in a cellar, Jadzia. Beaten if she spoke too loud. Starved if she disobeyed. Treated like an animal by the man who was supposed to protect her—while he stood in front of the Bajoran people and talked about order and civilization.

(Kira finally takes a drink, draining the glass.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
How am I supposed to hate her? She's a victim. The same as any of us.

DAX
Maybe you're not supposed to hate her.

KIRA
(frustrated)
Then what am I supposed to feel? She's Cardassian. Part of her, anyway. And that part—

DAX
That part didn't choose to be born any more than you chose to be born during the Occupation.

(Kira stares at her, something wounded in her expression.)

KIRA
That's not the same thing.

DAX
Isn't it?

(Before Kira can respond, QUARK appears, setting a fresh glass of springwine in front of her without being asked.)

QUARK
On the house.

KIRA
(suspicious)
Since when?

QUARK
Since you look like you need it, and I'm feeling generous.

KIRA
You're never generous.

QUARK
The forty-seventh Rule of Acquisition: "Don't trust a man who's not generous... unless he's Ferengi."

(Kira almost smiles despite herself. Quark doesn't leave. He leans against the bar, studying her with an expression that's surprisingly serious.)

QUARK (CONT'D)
I couldn't help overhearing.

KIRA
Of course you couldn't.

QUARK
It's a public establishment. Sound carries.

DAX
Quark...

QUARK
(ignoring her)
You want to know something, Major? Something I learned during my brief and deeply unpleasant time in Cardassian custody?

KIRA
I'm sure you're going to tell me whether I want to hear it or not.

QUARK
The Cardassians didn't just hurt Bajorans.

(Kira's expression hardens, but Quark presses on.)

QUARK (CONT'D)
They hurt anyone who got in the way of power. Including their own. Especially their own.

(He pauses, something flickering behind his eyes—an old memory, perhaps.)

QUARK (CONT'D)
Do you have any idea how many Cardassian citizens disappeared during the height of the Obsidian Order? How many families were destroyed because someone said the wrong thing, looked at the wrong person, had the wrong ancestor three generations back?

KIRA
That's different.

QUARK
Is it? The truth is, Major, tyranny doesn't care about species. It just cares about control. And the people who get crushed... they're all the same in the end. Broken. Afraid. Wondering what they did to deserve it.

(A long silence. Kira looks at him as if seeing him for the first time.)

KIRA
Since when do you know anything about tyranny?

QUARK
(shrugging)
I'm a businessman. I've done business with tyrants. You learn things.

(He straightens, pulling back into his familiar demeanor.)

QUARK (CONT'D)
Anyway. The springwine's still on the house. Don't tell anyone—it'll ruin my reputation.

(He moves away to attend to other customers. Kira stares after him, then down at her glass.)

DAX
(gently)
He's not wrong, you know.

KIRA
I know.

(She stands abruptly, leaving the untouched glass on the bar.)

DAX
Kira—

KIRA
I need to think.

(She starts to go, then stops, half-turning back.)

KIRA (CONT'D)
All my life, I knew who the enemy was. It was simple. Clean. The Cardassians hurt us, we hurt them back.

DAX
And now?

KIRA
(quietly)
Now I don't know anything.

(She walks out. Dax watches her go, concern evident on her face. After a moment, Quark reappears, collecting Kira's abandoned glass.)

QUARK
She didn't drink it.

DAX
She will. Eventually.

QUARK
(nodding toward the door)
She'll be all right, you know. The Major's tougher than she looks.

DAX
I know.

QUARK
(beat)
So. Since she's not using it, you want the springwine?

DAX
(faint smile)
Goodnight, Quark.

(She rises, leaving payment for her raktajino—and a generous tip. Quark watches her go, then looks down at the two abandoned glasses, his expression momentarily unguarded. He shakes it off, pockets the tip, and returns to work as the bar's ambient noise continues around him.)

FADE OUT.

INT. WARDROOM - DAY

(The senior staff sits around the conference table. SISKO stands at the head, arms crossed. ODO has a PADD in hand. DAX studies a display screen showing tactical data. BASHIR looks agitated, leaning forward in his chair. Through the viewport, a DOMINION CRUISER holds position off the station.)

SISKO
The Vorta's name is Keevan. He arrived twenty minutes ago with a formal extradition request... and a warship.

DAX
One warship. They could have brought a fleet.

ODO
Which suggests they're hoping for a diplomatic resolution.

BASHIR
Or they're testing us. Seeing how we respond to pressure.

SISKO
Either way, their patience won't last forever. Keevan has given us six hours to surrender Mira Dukat into Dominion custody.

(He moves to the viewport, studying the cruiser.)

SISKO (CONT'D)
The formal charge is murder. Three Jem'Hadar guards and a Vorta administrator during her escape from Cardassia Prime.

BASHIR
Murder? She was a prisoner trying to escape. That's self-defense by any reasonable definition.

ODO
The Dominion doesn't recognize that distinction, Doctor. To them, Jem'Hadar lives are property of the Founders. Destroying property is theft. Killing a Vorta is... well, that they take personally.

DAX
What happens if we hand her over?

SISKO
Keevan was quite specific. She'll face tribunal on a Dominion world. The verdict is predetermined.

(A heavy pause.)

BASHIR
Then we can't give her to them. It's that simple.

ODO
Nothing about this situation is simple.

(He stands, activating a wall display that shows Mira's shuttle trajectory and recovered data fragments.)

ODO (CONT'D)
I've spent the last four hours verifying her story. The shuttle's warp signature is consistent with a Cardassian origin. The damage patterns suggest pursuit by Jem'Hadar attack ships. And the data she's provided...

(He pauses, choosing his words carefully.)

ODO (CONT'D)
It's genuine. Ship deployments, supply routes, communication frequencies. All of it checks out against what we know.

DAX
That's exactly what worries me.

BASHIR
What do you mean?

DAX
(standing, moving to the display)

Collaboration with inferiors. Dukat's entire political position depends on presenting himself as a champion of Cardassian superiority. Mira contradicts that narrative simply by existing.

ODO
That's the implication.

SISKO
And now she's escaped, carrying information that could help us in this war, and the Dominion wants her back so they can execute her for the crime of wanting to be free.

SISKO (CONT'D)

(She shakes her head.)

DAX (CONT'D)
No. Children aren't responsible for their parents' sins. That's a principle worth defending, regardless of who it protects.

SISKO
Constable?

ODO

(A beat.)

ODO (CONT'D)
Whether her intelligence is trustworthy is a separate question.

SISKO
Doctor?

BASHIR

DAX
So it's your call.

SISKO
It's my call.

BASHIR
(relieved)

Understood.

Already done.

SISKO
The Dominion wants us to make a decision based on fear. Fear of their ships, their soldiers, their retribution.

SISKO (CONT'D)
I've made my decision based on something else. Now let's make sure we can live with the consequences.

Dismissed.

MIRA (CONT'D)

Where will you go?

MIRA
Somewhere quiet. Somewhere my name doesn't mean anything.

MIRA (CONT'D)

Then why—
